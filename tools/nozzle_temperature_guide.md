# Nozzle Temperature Guide

Recommended nozzle temperatures for optimal 3D printing results. Temperatures may vary by printer, filament brand, and print settings.

## Temperature Chart

| Material | Nozzle Temp (°C) | Bed Temp (°C) | Notes |
|----------|------------------|---------------|-------|
| **PLA** | 190-220 | 40-60 | Most common; easy to print |
| **PETG** | 230-260 | 60-80 | Good balance of strength and ease |
| **ABS** | 230-270 | 90-110 | Requires enclosure; prone to warping |
| **ASA** | 240-270 | 90-110 | UV resistant; similar to ABS |
| **TPU** | 210-230 | 30-50 | Flexible; print slowly |
| **Nylon** | 240-280 | 70-100 | Strong and durable; needs enclosure |
| **PC (Polycarbonate)** | 270-310 | 90-120 | Very strong; requires high temp |
| **PEEK** | 300-350 | 100-140 | High-performance; extruder limited |
| **PP (Polypropylene)** | 230-260 | 70-90 | Flexible and chemical resistant |
| **PMMA (Acrylic)** | 230-260 | 80-100 | Transparent; prone to cracking |
| **POM (Delrin)** | 210-240 | 80-100 | Low friction; requires enclosure |
| **PVB** | 210-235 | 50-70 | Optical clarity; requires post-process |
| **CPE** | 240-260 | 70-85 | Chemical resistant; Bristol |

## Temperature Ranges Explained

### Low Temperature (180-220°C)
- **PLA**: Entry-level material, low warping
- **TPU**: Flexible, requires slower speeds
- **POM**: Engineering plastic, low friction

### Medium Temperature (220-260°C)
- **PETG**: Balanced properties, good layer adhesion
- **ABS/ASA**: Petroleum-based, requires ventilation
- **PP**: Semi-flexible, chemical resistant
- **PMMA**: Transparent, good optical properties

### High Temperature (260-300°C)
- **Nylon**: Strong, durable, absorbs moisture
- **PC**: Very strong, heat resistant
- **CPE**: Chemical resistant alternative to PETG

### Very High Temperature (300°C+)
- **PEEK**: Extreme temperature resistance
- High-temp engineering applications

## Quick Reference

```
Low (180-220):    PLA, TPU, POM
Medium (220-260): PETG, ABS, ASA, PP, PMMA, PVB, CPE
High (260-300):   Nylon, PC
Very High (300+): PEEK
```

## Tips

- **Start in middle of range**: Adjust ±10°C based on results
- **Signals too hot**: Stringing, blobbing, nozzle drool
- **Signals too cold**: Under-extrusion, poor layer adhesion, clicking
- **Enclosure**: Required for ABS, ASA, Nylon, PC, PEEK to prevent warping
- **Nozzle material**: Use hardened steel for abrasives (CFE, glass-filled)
